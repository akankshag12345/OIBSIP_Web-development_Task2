Swami Vivekananda

"Arise, awake, and stop not till the goal is reached."

Biography

Swami Vivekananda (1863-1902) was an Indian Hindu monk, a chief disciple of the 19th-century saint Ramakrishna. He was a key figure in the introduction of Indian philosophies of Vedanta and Yoga to the Western world. Vivekananda is best known for his speech at the Parliament of the World's Religions in Chicago in 1893, which began with the iconic words, "Sisters and brothers of America...". He inspired generations to value education, discipline, and unity through his spiritual and nationalistic messages.

Quotes

  • "Take risks in your life. If you win, you can lead! If you lose, you can guide!"
  • "In a day, when you don't come across any problems—you can be sure that you are traveling in a wrong path."
  • "All power is within you; you can do anything and everything."
  • "You have to grow from the inside out. None can teach you, none can make you spiritual."

Achievements

  • Revival of Hinduism: He played a significant role in reviving Hinduism and showcasing its modern relevance, particularly at the Parliament of the World's Religions in Chicago (1893).
  • Introduction of Neo-Vedanta: Vivekananda introduced Neo-Vedanta, a Westernized interpretation of Hinduism that integrates spirituality with material progress.
  • Establishment of Ramakrishna Mission: He founded the Ramakrishna Mission (1897), a philanthropic and spiritual organization focused on promoting Vedanta teachings and social service.
  • Promotion of Education: Vivekananda advocated for an educational framework combining modern scientific knowledge with spiritual wisdom.
  • Advocacy for Social Reform: He championed social reforms, particularly in areas of caste discrimination and women's rights.
